# Daroda Accounting — CRM Discovery Questionnaire & Walkthrough Guide

## Purpose

This document helps gather the **minimum real‑world workflow information** needed to design a practical CRM plan for Daroda Accounting.

The goal is NOT to over‑engineer a system.

The goal is to understand how Rose actually works today so that any CRM:

- reduces stress
- prevents missed follow‑ups
- improves visibility into leads
- supports growth without adding complexity

---

## How to Use This Document

This is best done as a **live walkthrough conversation**, not just sending a list of questions.

Suggested framing:

> “I’m not trying to overcomplicate this. I just want to understand your real workflow so any CRM plan matches how you actually work instead of creating more stress.”

Encourage examples and real stories.

Avoid hypothetical answers.

---

# Core CRM Discovery Questions

## 1. What kinds of leads exist?

Examples may include:

- tax preparation
- bookkeeping
- cash flow planning
- speaking / workshop requests
- referrals from existing clients
- other

Why this matters:

Different lead types may require different follow‑up approaches or pipeline paths.

---

## 2. Walk me through what happens after someone first contacts you

Ask for the real current flow:

- Someone fills out a form or books a call → then what?
- Who responds?
- How quickly?
- What additional info is gathered?
- When do you decide they are a good fit?

This is often the **single most important CRM design insight.**

---

## 3. What are the real stages from lead to client?

Avoid inventing stages.

Ask:

- What steps actually happen between first contact and becoming a client?
- Where do people typically stall?
- When do you consider someone “qualified”?
- When do you consider them officially a “client”?

---

## 4. What information do you wish you had right away about a lead?

Examples:

- Name
- Email
- Phone
- Business type
- Service needed
- Referral source
- Urgency
- Notes
- Consultation date
- Status

Focus only on **information that is actually useful.**

---

## 5. How do you currently remember who needs follow‑up?

Examples:

- Email flags
- Calendar reminders
- Sticky notes
- Memory
- Spreadsheet
- Nothing structured

Ask:

> “Would you want the CRM to remind you automatically?”

---

## 6. Who would actually use the CRM?

Possibilities:

- Rose only
- Rose + staff
- Developer initially
- Future assistant / admin

This affects:

- permissions
- UI complexity
- SOP design
- dashboard layout

---

## 7. What would make you feel like the CRM is helping?

Examples:

- Fewer missed follow‑ups
- Better organization
- More conversions
- Clear visibility into pipeline
- Less stress

This answer should guide the entire CRM scope.

---

# Secondary but Valuable Questions

## 8. Do different lead types need different workflows?

Examples:

- Speaking inquiry vs tax prep
- Planner download vs referral
- High‑intent vs low‑intent

May require tags or multiple pipelines.

---

## 9. What should happen with “not a fit” or inactive leads?

Options:

- Archive
- Mark lost
- Revisit later
- Keep for marketing

This affects data cleanliness and reporting.

---

## 10. Do you want notes/history on every person?

If yes, CRM should support activity logging:

- Call happened
- Email sent
- Proposal discussed
- Meeting booked
- Converted to client

---

## 11. Do you want to track where leads come from?

Examples:

- Referral
- Google search
- Social media
- Speaking event
- Website planner download

This is extremely valuable for future growth decisions.

---

## 12. What does success look like from a reporting standpoint?

Examples:

- Leads per week
- Booked consultations
- Conversion rate
- Most requested services
- Follow‑ups overdue

Only build dashboards she will actually use.

---

## 13. Are there compliance or privacy boundaries?

Because this is an accounting practice:

Ask:

- What client data should NOT be stored in the CRM?
- Should financial data stay outside the CRM?
- Are there notes that should remain only in email or secure systems?

This defines safe system scope.

---

## Final Reminder

A good CRM for Daroda Accounting should:

- reflect real workflow
- reduce mental load
- support growth visibility
- remain simple to maintain

This questionnaire ensures the CRM plan is grounded in reality rather than assumptions.
